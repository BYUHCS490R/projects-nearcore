The Culinary Compass: A Community Recipe Database

1. Project Title and Topic

Title: The Culinary Compass

Topic: A website where people can share, look up, and save their favorite home-cooked meals, focusing on simple and diverse recipes.

2. Project Rationale (Brief Paragraph)

I picked "The Culinary Compass" because I want to build a useful site that helps bring people together through food. Most recipe sites are hard to use or focus on expensive meals. My goal is to create a clean, easy-to-use site where people can share simple, beloved family recipes. This will be a great resource for students and anyone learning to cook. This project lets me practice using forms, tables, and multiple pages, which is exactly what we've been learning in class.

3. Content Outline for Six Pages

Home Page (index.html)

  • Site name and tagline ("Navigate Your Next Meal").
  • A short welcome explaining what the site is for.
  • 3 special "Recipe of the Week" cards (with picture, name, and link).
  • A big button that says: "Submit Your Recipe."

Page 1: Submit a Recipe (form.html)

  • The main form where users add their recipes (fields listed in Section 4).
  • Tips on writing a good recipe.
  • A "thank you" message after a recipe is submitted.

Page 2: Find a Recipe (index.html, Search Focus)

  • A search bar to look for recipes.
  • Ways to filter recipes (like by Food Type, Meal Category, or Cooking Time).
  • A list of recipe cards to browse, showing the Recipe Name and who submitted it.

Page 3: Dessert Recipes

  • A short intro to the Desserts section.
  • A featured list of dessert recipes with details and pictures.
  • Different sections for baked, non-baked, and healthier desserts.

Page 4: Main Dishes

  • A short intro to the Main Dishes section.
  • A featured list of main dish recipes (chicken, vegetarian, etc.).
  • A focus on popular dishes from around the world.

Page 5: Cooking Measurements Table (Required Table Page)

  • A helpful article about common cooking measurements and how to convert them.
  • The required HTML table (details in Section 4).
  • Simple tips for measuring ingredients accurately.
