Cooking Measurements and Conversions

One of the hardest parts of cooking is getting the measurements right. Whether you're moving between **Standard (US) and Metric (International)** or just need to know how many teaspoons are in a cup, this guide is here to help. Using the correct measurements is key to recipe success.

Use the required HTML table below for quick, common **volume** conversions. *Data for this table is sourced from general cooking reference material.*

Common Liquid Volume Conversions
Standard Unit Metric Equivalent Fluid Ounces (fl oz) Cups
1 Teaspoon (tsp) 5 mL 0.17 1/48
1 Tablespoon (tbsp) 15 mL 0.5 1/16
1/4 Cup 60 mL 2 1/4
1 Cup 240 mL 8 1
1 Pint 475 mL 16 2

Simple Tips for Accurate Measuring

  • **Dry Ingredients:** Spoon flour into the measuring cup and level off with a straight edge.
  • **Liquid Ingredients:** Use transparent measuring cups and check the measurement line at eye level.
